Adèle Castillon

Adèle Castillon, born October 24, 2001 in Angers, is a French singer, actress, and YouTuber.

Biography

Adèle Castillon became known to the general public thanks to her videos with quirky humor, which she has published since the age of 13.

Very quickly spotted by Daphné Thavaud, creator of the Club Agence Artistique, she obtained her first film role in 2017 in Dominique Farrugia's film: Under the same roof and shared the poster with Gilles Lellouche and Louise Bourgoin.

The same year, she was sent to Dharamsala to interview the Dalai Lama. The conversation is at the origin of the book Make the Revolution! published in 2017, by Sofia Stril-Rever based on interviews with the 14th Dalai Lama. A documentary of this meeting was published on the Verticale platform.

In 2019, she plays a disturbing teenager in Sébastien Marnier's film L'Heure de la sortie where, in the company of her classmates, she torments Laurent Lafitte.

In September 2018, she created, with Matthieu Reynaud, Videoclub, an electropop group. Their first single Amour Plastique, produced by Régis Reynaud, met with some success. A first concert followed, which was sold out on April 11, 2019 in Nantes. The group begins a first tour in summer 2019. Adèle Castillon and Matthieu Reynaud perform notably at the Ulnatrofestival in Milan and at the Delta Festival9 in Marseille. They are also scheduled at the Cabourg mon amour festival, at the end of June
